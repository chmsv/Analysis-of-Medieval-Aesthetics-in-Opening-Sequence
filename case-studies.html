Case studies

The focus of the research is on identifying common features and similarities among the films in the database, whether it be a plot based on the same literary source or a common motif in the presentation and design of the opening title cards.
The study provides examples of films and breaks down instances of intentional or accidental convergence. In each case, an attempt was made to identify the reasons for this or that artistic decision, and the presence or absence of influence of the medieval and early modern book tradition on them.

To what extent did the art department rely on the plot of the motion picture, the time and place of action, and the director's vision when creating the opening credits card? Were the opening sequence considered as part of the narrative and did it support or reflect the story, or was it purely auxiliary in nature? To answer these and many other questions, here are the case studies presented.

The digital project is part of a master's thesis in the Rare Book and Digital Humanities program at the University of France-Comté .
